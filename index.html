About Me

Hi! I'm Eva Krueger, a robotics PhD student at Oregon State University. I graduated from Swarthmore College in 2024 with a BS in General Engineering and a BA in Environmental Studies. I’m passionate about robotics, particularly in agricultural automation systems, and I’m currently conducting research in manipulation and grasping for use in intelligent machines and materials.

Projects

  • Imaging Robot for Lettuce Production (Hippo Harvest): Developed a faster and higher-resolution imaging robot for a greenhouse lettuce farm. This robot captures images continuously, making it ten times faster than the previous version. It uses two Basler high-resolution cameras—one for RGB imaging and another for spectral imaging—plus a sensor for depth images.
  • Robotic Manipulation in Agricultural Automation: Working on grasping and manipulation techniques to optimize agricultural tasks using intelligent machines at Oregon State University.

Experience

  • Summer Robotics Intern - Hippo Harvest (June 2024 - Aug 2024): Created a new imaging robot for greenhouse lettuce farming, improving efficiency by capturing images on the move. Utilized advanced imaging techniques with RGB and spectral cameras.
  • Graduate Researcher - Oregon State University (Sept 2024 - Present): Conducting research in the Intelligent Machines and Materials lab under Professor Joseph Davidson, focusing on agricultural robotics and automation systems.
